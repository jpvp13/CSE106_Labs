"On-a-Budget" Webcam

Entry level webcam, good for basic video recording but with little to no color correct, focus or any other manual adjustment

Pros: Low cost, plug-n-play, long USB cable

Cons: 720p, low quality, grainy, no focus

Rating: 6/10

"On-a-Budget" DSLR (my current cam)

Entry level DSLR, good for general photograph, great for beginner streamers. Allows for control of all manual adjustments and high quality video

Pros: Easy to use, great video quality, can be used for other purposes, lots of after market equipment

Cons: higher-end of the budget picks, needs addition equipment to work for streaming (USB cable and DC to powerpak adapter)

Rating: 9/10

High End Stream webCam

Small, high quality streaming camera capable of capturing 1080p and with a larger field of view

Pros: good price range (normally on sale), HD 1080p, clips to monitor, cross-compatability with different OS's, two built in microphones

Cons: no manual adjustments (all software adjustments)

Rating: 8/10

Overkill Webcam

Small, high transfer speed webcam capable of 1080p60, professional level equipment

Pros: automatically keeps user in focus, type C connection, True FHD 1080p60

Cons: not budget friendly

Rating: 9/10
